Forty-eight hours after Hurricane Michael devastated Florida's Mexico Beach, residents of this once-idyllic beach community are beginning to come to grips with the reality that much of their town is simply gone.

Numerous homes in this resort town of about 1,200 were shattered into splinters or ripped from their foundations. One storm chaser said the area looked like a "nuclear bomb had gone off."

Rescuers and residents are struggling to return to the town devastated by a direct hit from the high-end Category 4 hurricane to assess the damage and search for the reported 285 people believed to have remained behind.
